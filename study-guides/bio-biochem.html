1. Amino Acids & Protein Structure

⚡ High Yield
Know all 20 amino acids by name, 1-letter code, side chain chemistry, and pKa values. The MCAT tests this constantly.

General Amino Acid Structure

All amino acids share an α-carbon bonded to: amino group (–NH₃⁺), carboxyl group (–COO⁻), hydrogen (–H), and a variable R-group (side chain). At physiological pH (7.4), the amino group is protonated and the carboxyl group is deprotonated.

Zwitterion at pH 7.4: ⁺H₃N — Cα(H)(R) — COO⁻

Protein Structure Levels

Primary (1°)

Linear sequence of amino acids linked by peptide bonds (–CO–NH–). The peptide bond has partial double-bond character and is planar. Determined by DNA sequence.

Secondary (2°)

α-helix: Right-handed coil; H-bonds between C=O of residue n and N-H of residue n+4. Each turn = 3.6 residues. Proline breaks helices. Hydrophobic residues on one face → amphipathic helices.

β-sheet: Parallel (same direction) or antiparallel (opposite direction); antiparallel is more stable due to better H-bond geometry.

Tertiary (3°)

3D folding of a single polypeptide. Stabilized by: hydrophobic interactions (major driving force), disulfide bonds (covalent), ionic interactions (salt bridges), H-bonds, van der Waals forces.

Quaternary (4°)

Association of 2+ polypeptide subunits. Example: hemoglobin (2α + 2β subunits). Stabilized by same forces as tertiary except no disulfide bonds between chains (typically).

Protein Denaturation

Loss of 3D structure without breaking peptide bonds. Caused by heat, extreme pH, reducing agents (break disulfide bonds), detergents. Usually irreversible in vivo.

2. Enzyme Kinetics

Michaelis-Menten Kinetics

v = Vmax[S] / (Km + [S])
  • Vmax: Maximum reaction velocity (when enzyme is saturated with substrate)
  • Km: Michaelis constant = [S] at which v = ½Vmax. Low Km = HIGH affinity
  • kcat: Catalytic rate constant (turnover number) — number of substrate molecules converted per enzyme per second
  • kcat/Km: Catalytic efficiency. Maximum value ≈ 10⁸–10⁹ M⁻¹s⁻¹ (diffusion limited)

Lineweaver-Burk Plot

Double-reciprocal plot: 1/v vs. 1/[S]. Linear form of Michaelis-Menten. Y-intercept = 1/Vmax; X-intercept = –1/Km; Slope = Km/Vmax.

Allosteric Regulation

Allosteric enzymes have multiple subunits and show sigmoidal kinetics (cooperative binding). Activators shift the curve left (↑ affinity); inhibitors shift right. Example: Hemoglobin (not an enzyme but shows cooperativity).

Feedback Inhibition

The end product of a metabolic pathway inhibits an early enzyme in the pathway. Classic example: ATP inhibits phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1) in glycolysis.

Pyruvate Dehydrogenase Complex (PDC)

Location: Mitochondrial matrix. Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA + CO₂ + NADH. Requires: TPP, lipoamide, CoA, FAD, NAD⁺.

Inhibited by: Acetyl-CoA, NADH, ATP. Activated by: Pyruvate, AMP, ADP, Ca²⁺.

Deficiency: Pyruvate builds up → lactic acidosis. Also causes neurological symptoms.

Electron Transport Chain & Oxidative Phosphorylation

Location: Inner mitochondrial membrane.

Complex I (NADH dehydrogenase): NADH → NAD⁺; pumps 4H⁺ Complex II (Succinate dehydrogenase): FADH₂ → FAD; no H⁺ pump Complex III (Cytochrome bc1): passes e⁻ via ubiquinol; pumps 4H⁺ Complex IV (Cytochrome c oxidase): O₂ → H₂O; pumps 2H⁺ ATP Synthase (Complex V): uses proton gradient to make ATP (≈3H⁺/ATP)

ATP yield: NADH ≈ 2.5 ATP · FADH₂ ≈ 1.5 ATP · Total per glucose ≈ 30–32 ATP

Inhibitors: Rotenone (Complex I), malonate (Complex II), antimycin A (Complex III), cyanide/CO (Complex IV), oligomycin (ATP synthase).

Uncouplers: DNP (dinitrophenol), thermogenin (UCP1 in brown fat) — dissipate proton gradient → heat instead of ATP.

Gluconeogenesis

Location: Liver (primarily), kidney cortex. Converts pyruvate/lactate/glycerol/amino acids → glucose. Activated by glucagon and cortisol. Inhibited by insulin.

Unique enzymes (bypass irreversible glycolysis steps):

  • Pyruvate carboxylase: Pyruvate → OAA (mitochondria; requires biotin, ATP)
  • PEPCK: OAA → PEP + CO₂ (requires GTP)
  • F1,6BPase: F1,6BP → F6P (bypasses PFK-1; inhibited by AMP, F2,6BP)
  • G6Pase: G6P → Glucose + Pᵢ (liver and kidney only; gives blood glucose)

Fatty Acid Oxidation (β-oxidation)

Location: Mitochondrial matrix. Fatty acids activated to acyl-CoA in cytoplasm, transported into mitochondria via carnitine shuttle (rate-limiting step; inhibited by malonyl-CoA).

Each cycle of β-oxidation: produces 1 FADH₂ + 1 NADH + 1 Acetyl-CoA (shortens chain by 2C).

Fatty Acid Synthesis

Location: Cytoplasm. Acetyl-CoA → malonyl-CoA (acetyl-CoA carboxylase, requires biotin) → palmitate (16C). Stimulated by insulin, citrate. Inhibited by glucagon.

Ketone Body Metabolism

Produced in liver mitochondria from acetyl-CoA during fasting/starvation: acetoacetate, β-hydroxybutyrate, acetone. Used by brain, muscle, heart as fuel. Liver cannot use ketones (lacks thiophorase/succinyl-CoA:acetoacetate CoA transferase).

4. Molecular Biology

DNA Structure & Replication

B-form DNA: right-handed double helix, 10 bp/turn, antiparallel strands. A pairs with T (2 H-bonds); G pairs with C (3 H-bonds).

Replication is semi-conservative (Meselson-Stahl experiment), bidirectional from multiple origins in eukaryotes.

Transcription

DNA → RNA by RNA polymerase. In prokaryotes: single RNA pol; sigma factor recognizes promoter (–10 and –35 elements); no processing needed.

In eukaryotes: RNA Pol II for mRNA; pre-mRNA processing: 5' 7-methylguanosine cap, 3' poly-A tail, splicing of introns by spliceosomes.

Promoter → Transcription Start Site (TSS) → Gene Template strand read 3'→5' → mRNA synthesized 5'→3'

Translation

mRNA → Protein at ribosomes. Genetic code: 64 codons (61 coding + 3 stop codons: UAA, UAG, UGA). Start codon: AUG (Met).

Ribosome sites: A (aminoacyl) site, P (peptidyl) site, E (exit) site. Peptide bond formed by peptidyl transferase (23S rRNA in bacteria).

Antibiotic Targets for the MCAT
Tetracyclines: block A site binding · Aminoglycosides: misreading · Macrolides (erythromycin): block translocation · Chloramphenicol: inhibits peptidyl transferase (50S) · Linezolid: inhibits 50S · Streptomycin: 30S

Gene Regulation

Lac operon (prokaryote): Negative control by lac repressor (bound to operator when glucose/no lactose) + positive control by CAP-cAMP (high cAMP when glucose is low → maximal transcription).

Eukaryotic regulation: Transcription factors, enhancers (can act at distance), silencers, chromatin remodeling (histone acetylation opens chromatin; methylation often silences).

DNA Repair

  • Base excision repair: Removes damaged bases (e.g., deaminated cytosine → uracil)
  • Nucleotide excision repair: Removes bulky lesions (pyrimidine dimers from UV light)
  • Mismatch repair: Corrects replication errors; defects → Lynch syndrome (hereditary colorectal cancer)
  • Double-strand break repair: Homologous recombination (accurate; uses sister chromatid) or NHEJ (error-prone; Ku proteins)

Cell Cycle

G₁ → S → G₂ → M (Mitosis) → [Cytokinesis] → G₁...
  • G₁: Cell growth; R (restriction) point. CDK4/6-Cyclin D phosphorylates Rb → releases E2F transcription factor → S phase entry

  • M checkpoint (spindle assembly): All chromosomes must be attached to spindle before anaphase

6. Genetics

Mendelian Genetics

  • Dominant: One allele sufficient for phenotype (capital letter: A)
  • Recessive: Both alleles needed (aa)
  • Incomplete dominance: Heterozygote shows intermediate phenotype (RW flower = pink)
  • Codominance: Both alleles expressed simultaneously (ABO blood groups)
  • Epistasis: One gene masks another (e.g., Labrador coat color)

Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium

p + q = 1    p² + 2pq + q² = 1

p = frequency of dominant allele; q = frequency of recessive allele; q² = frequency of affected individuals (recessive). Assumes no mutation, migration, selection, genetic drift, random mating.

X-linked Inheritance

X-linked recessive: Sons of carrier mother have 50% chance of being affected. Daughters of affected father are all carriers. Males affected more (hemizygous). Examples: hemophilia A (Factor VIII), hemophilia B (Factor IX), Duchenne muscular dystrophy, G6PD deficiency, red-green color blindness.

7. Organ Systems & Physiology

Cardiovascular System

Heart sounds: S1 = mitral/tricuspid closure (lub); S2 = aortic/pulmonic closure (dub).

Cardiac output: CO = HR × SV. SV increased by: ↑ preload (Frank-Starling), ↑ contractility, ↓ afterload.

Blood pressure: BP = CO × TPR. Autoregulation maintains organ perfusion.

Vessels: Arteries (high pressure, thick wall) → arterioles (resistance vessels) → capillaries (exchange) → venules → veins (reservoir, high capacitance).

Respiratory System

Lung volumes: TV (tidal volume ~500mL) + IRV + ERV = VC (vital capacity ~4.8L). FRC = ERV + RV. TLC = VC + RV.

Gas exchange: Follows Fick's law. CO₂ transported mainly as bicarbonate (70%), hemoglobin bound (23%), dissolved (7%).

Oxygen-hemoglobin dissociation: Sigmoidal curve. Right shift (↓ O₂ affinity) = Bohr effect: ↑ CO₂, ↑ H⁺, ↑ temp, ↑ 2,3-BPG. Fetal Hb (HbF) has ↑ O₂ affinity (shifts curve left) due to γ-subunits not binding 2,3-BPG well.

Renal System

Nephron segments: Glomerulus → Proximal tubule (reabsorbs ~65% Na⁺, all glucose/amino acids, HCO₃⁻) → Loop of Henle (establishes concentration gradient) → Distal tubule (regulated reabsorption) → Collecting duct (ADH-regulated water reabsorption).

GFR: Filtered by glomerulus; regulated by afferent/efferent arteriole tone. Normal ~125 mL/min.

Hormones: ADH (↑ water reabsorption) · Aldosterone (↑ Na⁺, ↑ K⁺ secretion) · Atrial natriuretic peptide (↓ Na⁺ reabsorption) · Renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system.

Nervous System

Neuron types: Sensory (afferent), Motor (efferent), Interneurons.

Action potential: Depolarization (Na⁺ influx via voltage-gated Na⁺ channels → threshold) → Repolarization (K⁺ efflux) → Hyperpolarization → Return to resting (-70 mV). Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase restores ionic balance.

Synaptic transmission: Action potential → Ca²⁺ influx → vesicle fusion → neurotransmitter release → receptor binding → postsynaptic potential (EPSP or IPSP).

Divisions: CNS (brain + spinal cord) · PNS: Somatic (voluntary) + Autonomic (sympathetic + parasympathetic).
