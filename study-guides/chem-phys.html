Chemical Bonding

Ionic bonds: Metal + nonmetal; electron transfer; high melting points; conduct in solution.

Covalent bonds: Nonmetal + nonmetal; electron sharing. Polar covalent = unequal sharing (ΔEN 0.4–1.7). Nonpolar = equal sharing or ΔEN < 0.4.

VSEPR: Electron groups repel → geometry. Lone pairs cause greater repulsion than bonds. Lone pairs compress bond angles.

Gas Laws

Ideal Gas Law: PV = nRT (R = 8.314 J/mol·K = 0.0821 L·atm/mol·K) Boyle's Law: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂ (constant T, n) Charles's Law: V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂ (constant P, n) Gay-Lussac's Law: P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂ (constant V, n) Combined: P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂ Dalton's Law: P_total = P₁ + P₂ + ... (partial pressures) Graham's Law: Rate₁/Rate₂ = √(M₂/M₁) (effusion rates)

Real gases deviate from ideal at high pressure and low temperature. Van der Waals equation corrects for intermolecular forces (a) and molecular volume (b).

Solution Chemistry

van 't Hoff factor (i): NaCl → i = 2; CaCl₂ → i = 3; glucose → i = 1

2. Thermodynamics & Kinetics

Laws of Thermodynamics

  • Zeroth Law: Thermal equilibrium is transitive
  • First Law: Energy is conserved: ΔU = q + w (w = –PΔV for expansion)
  • Second Law: ΔS_universe ≥ 0 (entropy always increases for spontaneous processes)
  • Third Law: S = 0 for a perfect crystal at 0 K

Gibbs Free Energy

ΔG = ΔH − TΔS ΔG° = −RT ln K → ΔG° = −nFE° ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln Q

Hess's Law & Enthalpy

ΔH°rxn = Σ ΔH°f(products) − Σ ΔH°f(reactants) Bond enthalpies: ΔH = Σ bonds broken (reactants) − Σ bonds formed (products)

Chemical Kinetics

Rate = k[A]^m[B]^n (m, n = reaction orders determined experimentally) Arrhenius: k = Ae^(−Ea/RT) → ln(k) = ln(A) − Ea/RT First order: [A] = [A]₀e^(−kt); t₁/₂ = 0.693/k Second order: 1/[A] = 1/[A]₀ + kt; t₁/₂ = 1/(k[A]₀)

Equilibrium

K = [products]^stoich / [reactants]^stoich (gases: Kp vs Kc: Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn) Le Chatelier's Principle: System opposes changes to restore equilibrium

Ksp = solubility product. Common insoluble salts: AgCl, BaSO₄, CaCO₃, PbS. Common ion effect decreases solubility.

3. Acids, Bases & Buffers

Key Definitions

  • Arrhenius: Acid donates H⁺ to water; base produces OH⁻
  • Brønsted-Lowry: Acid = proton donor; Base = proton acceptor
  • Lewis: Acid = electron pair acceptor; Base = electron pair donor (broader)
Kw = [H⁺][OH⁻] = 1×10⁻¹⁴ at 25°C pH = –log[H⁺] pOH = –log[OH⁻] pH + pOH = 14 Ka × Kb = Kw → pKa + pKb = 14

Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation

pH = pKa + log([A⁻]/[HA]) For a buffer: pH ≈ pKa when [A⁻] = [HA] (1:1 ratio)
⚡ Buffer Rule
Effective buffering range = pKa ± 1 pH unit. Best buffer = weak acid + conjugate base (or weak base + conjugate acid). Phosphate buffer (pKa 6.8) is physiologically important.

Titration Concepts

  • Equivalence point: Moles of acid = moles of base. pH depends on the salt formed.
  • Half-equivalence point: pH = pKa (for weak acid titrated with strong base)
  • Strong acid + Strong base: Equivalence at pH 7
  • Weak acid + Strong base: Equivalence pH > 7 (conjugate base is basic)
  • Weak base + Strong acid: Equivalence pH < 7 (conjugate acid is acidic)

Amino Acid Titration & pI

pI (isoelectric point) = (pKa1 + pKa2) / 2 For basic AAs (His, Lys, Arg): pI = (pKa2 + pKa3) / 2 For acidic AAs (Asp, Glu): pI = (pKa1 + pKa2) / 2

4. Electrochemistry

Oxidation-Reduction

OIL RIG: Oxidation Is Loss; Reduction Is Gain (of electrons). Oxidizing agent is reduced; reducing agent is oxidized.

Electrochemical cell: Anode = oxidation (A = an = anion flows toward); Cathode = reduction.

E°cell = E°cathode − E°anode (standard cell potential) ΔG° = −nFE° (n = moles of electrons; F = 96,485 C/mol) Nernst equation: E = E° − (RT/nF)ln Q ≈ E° − (0.0592/n)log Q at 25°C

Stereochemistry

Chiral center: Carbon with 4 different groups. R/S configuration assigned by Cahn-Ingold-Prelog priority rules.

Enantiomers: Non-superimposable mirror images; opposite R/S at all chiral centers. Same physical/chemical properties except optical activity.

Diastereomers: Stereoisomers that are NOT mirror images; different R/S at some (not all) chiral centers. Different physical and chemical properties.

Meso compounds: Contains chiral centers but has an internal plane of symmetry → achiral overall.

Racemic mixture: 50:50 mixture of enantiomers; no net optical activity.

6. Physics

⚡ Essential Equations to Memorize
Memorize ALL equations below. The MCAT provides no equation sheet.

Mechanics

Kinematics (constant acceleration): v = v₀ + at x = v₀t + ½at² v² = v₀² + 2ax x = ½(v + v₀)t Newton's Laws: F_net = ma (1N = 1 kg·m/s²) Weight: W = mg (g = 9.8 m/s² ≈ 10 m/s²) Friction: f = μN (static μ_s > kinetic μ_k) Work & Energy: W = Fd cos θ (only parallel component) KE = ½mv² PE_gravity = mgh PE_spring = ½kx² (Hooke's Law: F = –kx) Power: P = W/t = Fv Momentum: p = mv Impulse: J = FΔt = Δp Conservation: p_before = p_after (elastic and inelastic) Elastic collision: KE conserved; Inelastic: KE not conserved

Fluid Mechanics

Density: ρ = m/V (water: 1000 kg/m³ = 1 g/cm³) Pressure: P = F/A = ρgh (hydrostatic) Buoyancy: F_b = ρ_fluid × V_submerged × g (Archimedes) Continuity: A₁v₁ = A₂v₂ (flow rate Q = Av) Bernoulli: P₁ + ½ρv₁² + ρgh₁ = P₂ + ½ρv₂² + ρgh₂ Poiseuille: Q = πr⁴ΔP / 8ηL (flow is very sensitive to radius!) Resistance: R = 8ηL/πr⁴ (like Ohm's law for fluids)
Clinical Relevance
Arteriosclerosis = ↓ r → dramatically ↑ R and ↓ Q. Radius is the most important factor in vascular resistance.

Waves & Sound

v = fλ (wave speed = frequency × wavelength) Sound: v_sound ≈ 340 m/s in air; faster in solids/liquids Intensity: I = P/A (W/m²); I ∝ 1/r² (inverse square law) Decibels: β = 10 log(I/I₀) (I₀ = 10⁻¹² W/m²) Doppler effect: f_obs = f_source × (v ± v_obs)/(v ∓ v_source) (+ if approaching, – if receding) Standing waves (string): λₙ = 2L/n → fₙ = nv/2L Open pipe: fₙ = nv/2L (n = 1,2,3...) Closed pipe: fₙ = nv/4L (n = 1,3,5... odd harmonics only)

Optics

Snell's Law: n₁sin θ₁ = n₂sin θ₂ Total internal reflection: when θ > θ_c where sin θ_c = n₂/n₁ (n₁ > n₂) Thin lens equation: 1/f = 1/d_o + 1/d_i Magnification: m = –d_i/d_o = h_i/h_o Power (diopters): P = 1/f (meters) Converging (convex) lens: f > 0; can form real or virtual images Diverging (concave) lens: f < 0; always virtual, upright, reduced

Electricity & Magnetism

Coulomb's Law: F = kq₁q₂/r² (k = 9×10⁹ N·m²/C²) Electric field: E = F/q = kQ/r² (N/C = V/m) Electric potential: V = kQ/r (Volts) Potential energy: U = qV = kq₁q₂/r Capacitance: C = Q/V = ε₀A/d (F = Farads) Energy stored: U = ½CV² = Q²/2C Ohm's Law: V = IR (R in Ohms Ω) Power: P = IV = I²R = V²/R Series: R_total = R₁ + R₂... ; C_total = 1/(1/C₁ + 1/C₂...)
